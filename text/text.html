During a typical lifespan, a person spends a total of about six years dreaming[42] (which is about two hours each night).[43] Most dreams only last 5 to 20 minutes. During most dreams, the person dreaming is not aware that they are dreaming, no matter how absurd or eccentric the dream is. The reason for this may be that the prefrontal cortex, the region of the brain responsible for logic and planning, exhibits decreased activity during dreams.
